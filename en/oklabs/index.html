Code for Germany teams meet regularly in 13 cities. The teams are linked locally and globally. They exchange information on a regular basis, receive input and support from mentors and work together on code and applications. Every lab consists of members and lab leads. The leads organize meetings, manage communication channels, serve as points of contact and as a hub to the broader network. In January 2015, five more OK Labs joined the network.

The OK Labs are independent groups with their own communication channels like websites, etherpads, or Twitter accounts. The network and lab projects are presented on the codefor.de website. For details on groups and projects, it's worth taking a closer look at the websites and pads of individual groups. You can also stay informed by directly joining the mailing list of respective labs.
